# Example — Research task

## Raw prompt

```
tell me about the history of jiu-jitsu in Brazil
```

## Issues with this prompt

- No persona assigned
- No depth or audience specified
- No format declared
- No length specified
- No specific angle or focus

## Enhanced output (via `/enhance`)

> You are a martial arts historian specialising in Japanese and Brazilian grappling traditions, with academic familiarity with primary sources on the Gracie family, Mitsuyo Maeda, and the development of Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu through the 20th century.
>
> **Task:** Write a structured historical overview of the development of Jiu-Jitsu in Brazil from its arrival via Mitsuyo Maeda in the early 1910s through to the establishment of BJJ as a global sport.
>
> **Audience:** A serious BJJ practitioner and coach with intermediate knowledge of the sport who wants to deepen their understanding of where the art came from and why it evolved as it did.
>
> **Format and length:** Markdown article, approximately 2000 words, structured under these headers:
> 1. Origins — Maeda's journey and arrival in Belém
> 2. The Gracie family adoption and adaptation
> 3. The development of the Gracie challenge and early vale tudo
> 4. The split between Gracie Jiu-Jitsu and broader BJJ
> 5. The IBJJF era and globalisation
> 6. Key technical and philosophical differences from Japanese Jiu-Jitsu
>
> **Reasoning approach:** Think through this step by step. Before writing, identify the three or four pivotal moments that most shaped the art's trajectory. Structure the narrative around those turning points rather than a pure chronology.
>
> **Background context to weave in where relevant:**
> - The influence of Kodokan judo on Maeda's grappling style
> - The socioeconomic context of early 20th century Brazil that allowed BJJ to flourish
> - The role of Carlos and Hélio Gracie specifically
> - The eventual emergence of competitors like Carlson Gracie's lineage and the non-Gracie schools
>
> **Failure guards:**
> - Where there is historical dispute (and there is, particularly around the Gracie family's narrative versus academic accounts), present both views rather than picking one
> - Do not attribute specific quotes to Maeda or the Gracies unless you can identify the documented source
> - Distinguish between confirmed historical record and Gracie family oral tradition
